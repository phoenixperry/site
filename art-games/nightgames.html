Nightgames

NightGames(2013) encourages players to make musicand enter into an imaginary sonic world. A playspace is defined by four speakers housed in towers at the edges, and a central sculpture illuminated by interactive lighting. In addition, sculptures made of plexiglass pyramids illuminate the room. Beneath the entire sculpture, a steady rhythmic drum beat pulsates acting as a canvas onto which players can layer their sonic input via PS Move controllers and custom wearable interfaces. The speed of the beat and complexity of the sonic world reflects the number of players and the relationship of their gestures to each other.

The system rewards close collaboration with a richer melodic score and reactive lighting. Through the sonification and visualization of social interaction, NightGames brings a conscious awareness to players that individuals impact and form a collective environment.

This project seeks to further expand the public’s understanding of game play and theatre to include freeform interaction. This interaction design represent part of a deeper research endeavor to innovate controller mappings for games that embrace all bodies and abilities. By crafting a playspace that rewards many kinds of interaction, these objects will have affordances for many possible ways of moving. As a person who has struggled on an off with partial disability, Phoenix Perry passionately explores alternative computational interactions.

Made in collaboration with Margaret Schedel, David Last, and Adelle Lin.
