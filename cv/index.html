01Research Profile

An interdisciplinary researcher, educator and practitioner working at the intersections of human–computer interaction, creative computing and machine learning for embodied and artistic interaction design.

An artist and activist who uses culture as a medium, Phoenix is Research Lead on the AHRC BRAID UK project Federated Data Commons for Creative Communities and a named investigator on funded projects with a combined full economic cost exceeding £850,000. In 2026 they were competitively selected for the AHRC Doing AI Differently international research sandpit in Montreal, where they are co-developing the Anti-Gravity Machine with a UK–Canada cohort. Their open-source machine-learning tools (InteractML) are used by more than 60,000 developers, and they founded the Code Liberation Foundation, which taught creative computing to 6,000 women between 2012 and 2020. They have authored or co-authored more than 15 peer-reviewed papers and contributed to four books, with new 2026 work at NIME, ACM Creativity & Cognition and CHI PLAY, and completed their PhD at Goldsmiths in 2024. They are a Senior Fellow of the Higher Education Academy.

Research interests — human–computer interaction; games, play and playful interaction; embodied and physical interaction design; interactive and creative machine learning; responsible and generative AI; accessible (“crip”) technology and disability-led design; new interfaces for musical expression; movement and immersive media; and open-source tools that put machine learning in the hands of creative practitioners.
