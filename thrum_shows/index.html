About the piece

Hearts, in a room, finding a shared pulse

Two to six people wear heart-rate sensors. The room turns each pulse into light, vibration, and sound, and the group listens as separate rhythms drift, respond, and sometimes synchronise.

Resonant Thrum is part of an ongoing research project into embodiment and interpersonal synchrony and how bodies in the same space quietly influence one another. Each 10-minute session is both an artwork you experience and a small contribution to that research.

What to expect

Before you sign up

The format

You'll sit on a meditation cushion on the floor for 3–5 minutes during the piece, then take part in a short 3–4 minute discussion about your experience afterwards.

Heart-rate data

A sensor you will wear on your arm reads your pulse during the session. Readings are recorded and anonymised for research.

Photography & recording

The group may be photographed, audio- or video-recorded for documentation and academic presentation.

It's voluntary

Taking part is entirely voluntary. You can withdraw at any time, before or during the session, no reason needed.

You'll confirm your consent to the above when you reserve a slot.
