## 2. Thematic grouping

Four curatorial clusters. A few works bridge two themes — noted in _italics_.

### 🌱 Theme A — More-than-Human Ecologies
Nature, plants, microbes, materials and care extended across species.
- **Hyphae of Kindness** (Kumar) — mycelial care networks
- **The Blooming Archive** (Zhao) — human–plant relations, decolonial posthumanism
- **PlaSphere** (Ahn) — plastiglomerates, coastal ecology, AI-generated organisms
- **CONTACT** (Hieronymi) — microbiome & climate change through embodied play
- _**Rock Talk** (Lee) — AI community garden; bridges A ↔ D (care/community)_

### 🫁 Theme B — Bodies, Breath & Biofeedback
Physiological signals, ritual, and the felt body as interface.
- **Spirit** (Chen) — breath-driven tech-ritual with robotic vacuums
- **Breathe Kritisk** (Koowattanataworn) — breath, trust and clinical control
- **Resident Thrum** — group cardiac synchronisation
- **The Body & the Counter-Archive** (Hedayati) — biosensing + sound; _bridges B ↔ socio-political_
- _**I Dream a Lot but I Never Sleep** (Jiang) — embodied intimacy; bridges B ↔ C_

### 🤖 Theme C — AI, Authorship & Critical Machines
Generative AI interrogated for ethics, bias, authorship and its blind spots.
- **Artist-in-the-Loop** (Almeda) — hand-painting vs. generative image production
- **Cradle of Success** (Ray LC) — fortune-telling, stereotypes & generative AI
- **World Simulator** (Cole) — the limits/blindness of AI "world simulators"
- **Adversarial Aesthetics** (Williams) — steganography & data-poisoning as artist resistance
- **Inter:Lace** (Cai) — outsourcing social self-regulation to AI

### 🛋️ Theme D — Everyday, Slow & Speculative Tech
Long-lived, critical and speculative alternatives to consumer technology.
- **Portfolio of Research Products** (Boucher / IRS) — two decades of slow, field-ready artefacts
- **Bend To** (VR) — speculative immersive experience
- _**I Dream a Lot but I Never Sleep** (Jiang) — speculative companionship; bridges C/B ↔ D_

**Logic**
- **Open atrium spine** = self-running, daylight-tolerant, audio-isolated-by-headphones works. Visitors flow past them; these carry Themes A & D and are the social "front of house."
- **Dark Room 1 (Deep AV)** = projection/multichannel pieces that need low light and focused audio (Williams, Ahn, Hieronymi, Ray LC). Cluster the AI-critique + ecology AV works for a contemplative loop.
- **Dark Room 2 (Embodied black-box)** = sound-isolated, body-signal works (Resident Thrum needs strict isolation; Chen's recessed projection sits here too).
- **Performance / scheduled zone** = facilitated, time-slotted works (Hedayati, Koowattanataworn, Almeda, Bend To). Put a sign-up board here; these need a host and seclusion.
- Keep **Resident Thrum** and **Williams/Ahn** acoustically apart — all are audio-primary.
- **Zhao's** internet dependency and **Boucher's** no-internet preference are non-conflicting; just ensure a drop near Zhao.
